Tim Berners-Lee, born 1955, was a British computer scientist most known for his invention of the World Wide Web, URLs, and the coding language HTML. After he graduated, he would soon become a consultant at CERN, where he created a program named Enquire that could store information in files that contained connections both wthin and out files, soon being renamed to hypertext. Later, he started developing procedures that allowed computers to communicate with each other.
